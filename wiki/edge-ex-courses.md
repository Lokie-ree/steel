## Unit maps

### Algebra II (10 units)

1. Relationships Between Quantities — dimensional analysis, 1-var equations, function ops/inverses, rate of change, linear systems (incl. 3×3)
2. Quadratics and Complex Numbers — quadratic functions/inequalities, complex numbers & ops, completing the square, quadratic formula, transformations, square root functions, mixed-degree systems
3. Polynomials — cubes, division (long/synthetic), binomial theorem, rational roots, complex roots, quadratic-in-form, graphing, solving with technology
4. Rational Functions — simplify, equations, asymptotes/holes, graphing, inequalities
5. Radical Functions I — graphing radicals, nonperfect roots, rational exponents, add/subtract radicals
6. Radical Functions II — multiply/divide radicals, radical equations & extraneous roots, **Performance Task: Roller Coaster Design**
7. Exponential and Logarithmic Functions — graphing both, evaluating logs, base e, solving, modeling, rewriting
8. Sequences and Series — arithmetic/geometric, summation, series, recursion, modeling
9. Statistics and Probability — study design, representing data, std deviation, distributions, normal curve, inference, hypothesis testing
10. Trig, Nonlinear Functions, and Modeling — standard position, radians, unit circle, six trig functions, sine/cosine graphs & transformations, periodic modeling, absolute value / piecewise / step functions, modeling with functions

### Algebra III (8 units)

1. Equations and Inequalities I — multistep/literal equations, problem solving, linear systems (graphical, elimination, substitution, 3×3), inequalities (compound, two-variable)
2. Equations and Inequalities II — quadratics (factoring, completing square, formula), factoring completely, quadratic-in-form, rational exponents, radical equations
3. Lines, Functions, Graphs I — slope, parallel/perpendicular proofs, writing linear equations, comparing functions, function ops, **difference quotient**, analyzing graphs
4. Lines, Functions, Graphs II — piecewise, transformations of functions, linear functions, quadratic functions (standard/factored form), quadratic modeling
5. Polynomial/Rational/Inverse I — multiply/divide polynomials, synthetic division, rational roots, **fundamental theorem of algebra**, complex roots, graphing, polynomial inequalities
6. Polynomial/Rational/Inverse II — rational expressions/equations/graphs/inequalities, asymptotes, inverses (incl. domain restriction)
7. Exp & Log I — laws of exponents, graphing exponentials, rewriting, solving by rewriting base, base e, **regression models**, **exponential/logistic/logarithmic models**
8. Exp & Log II — graphing logs, properties of logarithms, solving exp/log equations, modeling

- Graphing Exponential Functions · Graphing Logarithmic Functions · Solving Log Equations Using Technology · Base e · Solving Exp and Log Equations · Modeling with Exp and Log Equations · Rewriting Exponential Functions

**Implication:** one interactive built for a shared lesson serves both preps. Highest-reuse
concepts across all units (each touched by many lessons in both courses):

1. **Function transformations** (`F-BF.B.3`) — cited by ~10 lessons spanning quadratics, radicals, exp/log, trig, absolute value. A single transformation explorer covers all of them.
2. **Graphing function families with key features** (`F-IF.B.4`, `F-IF.C.7.*`) — intercepts, asymptotes, end behavior, zeros.
3. **Polynomial zeros/factoring → graph** (`A-APR.B.2/B.3`).
4. **Exp/log equivalence and modeling** (`F-LE.A.4`, `A-SSE.B.3.c`).
5. Algebra II-only marquee pieces: **unit circle** (`F-TF.A.1/A.2`), **normal distribution** (`S-ID.A.4`), **sequences/series** (`F-BF.A.2`).
